## Wordmark — Construction

The wordmark is a measurement.

- **71** set in Space Grotesk Medium, `px` set in JetBrains Mono Medium at 55% of the cap height, baseline-shifted up by 5%. Together they read as a CSS unit. The joke is the brand.

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Display Face | Space Grotesk |
| Mono Face | JetBrains Mono |
| PX Scale | 0.55 × cap |
| Baseline Shift (px) | −5% |
| Tracking (71) | −40 |
| Min Size, Digital | 24px |
| Min Size, Print | 9mm |
| Clear Space | = height of "1" |

**Construction Grid:** 20 × 20 px

## Typography — Display

**Face:** Space Grotesk (Variable)
**Source:** fonts.google.com/specimen/Space+Grotesk — Free · OFL
**Style:** Medium 500 · Tracking −0.04em

| Scale | Size | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Hero | 188px | Title |
| — | 120px | Title |
| — | 80px | Section |
| Subtitle | 56px | Subtitle |

### Rules
- **Tracking:** Always negative on display. −0.04em default. −0.05em at 120px+.
- **Casing:** Sentence case. Title case never. ALL CAPS only in micro-labels.
- **Italics:** Display Italic is forbidden. For italic, switch to Instrument Serif.
- **Hyphenation:** Off. Break lines manually if you must.
- **Weight:** Medium 500 always. Bold 700 reserved for the wordmark.

## Typography — Body, Editorial, Mono

One body, one *editorial*, one mono.

### Body — Inter (Variable)
**Source:** Free · OFL · Variable
- **Sizes:** 13 / 15 / 17 / 18px
- **Weights:** Regular 400 · Medium 500
- **Line Height:** 1.45 body · 1.3 lead
- **Tracking:** −0.085em
- Never above 18px on the body; never below 13. Sentence case. Tracking 0. That's the whole point.

### Editorial — Instrument Serif (Italic)
**Source:** Free · OFL · Variable · Italic
- **Style:** Italic, Regular 400
- **Sizes:** +10–15% over the run-on display
- **Frequency:** Once per spread
- **Color:** Inherit from context
- Reserved for one word per page, maximum. The italic accent inside an otherwise grotesk paragraph. If you're using it twice on a page, you're using it wrong.

### Mono — JetBrains Mono
**Source:** Free · OFL · Variable
- **Casing:** UPPERCASE for labels
- **Tracking:** +0.06em on labels
- **Weight:** Medium 500
- Used for: timestamps, project tags, hex codes, footer metadata, the running header on every page of this guide.

## Color — Condiment Principle

Accents are condiments, not fills.

Pixel Red is the salt. You season the page. You don't drown the plate. One word, one cursor, one hover state. If you can't decide what gets the red, the answer is *nothing* on this spread.

### Permitted Red Uses
1. One word in a paragraph
2. Hover state on links
3. The cursor on case studies
4. A single underline, occasionally
5. A 1px rule, occasionally

### Correct vs Incorrect

✅ **Correct:** 1 word red · 17 words ink · 1 surface · accent = 5%

❌ **Incorrect:** All red · accent > 100% · loses meaning · reads as decoration

## Motion

Three rules. *Movement*, not decoration.

Scroll-jacking, parallax wallpaper, and "every section fades in" are why most agency websites feel the same. We don't.

### Principle 01 — One Signature Moment
Every page has exactly one thing that moves and is remembered. Not five. One. The hero, or the transition, or the cursor — pick.
- Frame Still · Type Morph · 400ms

### Principle 02 — The Cursor Is a Brand Asset
It shrinks on text. Expands on case studies. Turns Pixel Red on links. Move your mouse around this PDF.
- Default · 18px
- On Text · T-beam
- Link · Pixel Red

### Principle 03 — Transitions Over Scroll
Page transitions matter. Curtain wipe, type morph, color flash. Scroll animations don't — they're just showing off.
- Curtain Wipe · 480ms · Ease-Out Cubic

Confident. Dry. *Slightly cheeky.*

### Rules
1. Short sentences. One idea each.
2. Confidence without bragging. Show, don't boast.
3. Dry humor lives in the small places — 404, signatures.

## Don'ts

A brand is what you refuse as much as what you make. Six things this brand never does.

1. **Bullet lists** — If you're reaching for a list, you haven't edited the sentence yet. Use sentences. Use rules. Lists are a copywriting failure mode dressed up as structure.
2. **Agency-speak** — Synergy, partner with, unlock, holistic, world-class, leverage. Banned. Strike them on sight, including this one.
3. **Two accents at once** — Pixel Red and Cobalt and Acid in the same surface is a rave flyer. Pick one. Per project.
4. **Indian visual cues** — No mehndi, no marigold, no temple bell, no chai tropes. India is a fact about us. It is not a theme. Pure international craft.
5. **Pure white backgrounds** — White is too clean, too clinical, too Stripe. Paper #F2F0EC reads as paper. We are paper, not screen.
6. **Generic stock photography** — Two strangers shaking hands in front of a window. A laptop on a wooden desk with succulents. If we can't make it, we don't show it.
